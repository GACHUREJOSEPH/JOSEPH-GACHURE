Githeri with Avocado

A hearty and nutritious traditional Kenyan dish of boiled maize and beans, elevated with creamy avocado for extra richness. This wholesome meal is packed with protein and healthy fats, perfect for a satisfying lunch or dinner.

Ingredients

Main Components

  • 2 cups dried maize (corn) kernels
  • 1 cup dried beans (red kidney or pinto)
  • 1 large red onion, diced
  • 2 ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 cups water

For Serving

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1 lemon (for juice)
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley for garnish
  • Chili flakes (optional)

Instructions

Preparation

  1. Rinse the maize and beans thoroughly under running water.
  2. Soak the maize and beans separately in plenty of water overnight (or at least 8 hours).
  3. Drain and rinse the soaked maize and beans.

Cooking

  1. In a large pot, combine the soaked maize and beans with 4 cups of water.
  2. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover and simmer for 1 hour or until tender.
  3. In a separate pan, heat oil and sauté onions until translucent.
  4. Add garlic and tomatoes, cooking until tomatoes break down into a sauce.
  5. Add this mixture to the cooked maize and beans, stirring well.
  6. Season with salt and simmer for another 10 minutes.

Serving

  1. Cut avocados in half, remove pits, and slice flesh while still in skin.
  2. Scoop out avocado slices with a spoon and arrange on top of githeri.
  3. Squeeze fresh lemon juice over the avocado to prevent browning.
  4. Garnish with fresh cilantro or parsley.
  5. Serve hot with chili flakes on the side for those who prefer spice.

Nutritional Information

Per serving (1 bowl): Calories: 420 | Protein: 15g | Carbs: 60g | Fiber: 16g | Healthy Fats: 15g

Chef's Tips

  • Time-saving: Use canned maize and beans to reduce cooking time to 20 minutes.
  • Variations: Add chopped spinach or kale for extra nutrients.
  • Storage: Keeps well in fridge for 3 days. Avocado is best added fresh when serving.
  • Traditional touch: Some Kenyans enjoy this with a side of fermented milk (mursik).
